Open States

Opening access to U.S. State level legislative data

So, who are we?

James Turk (project lead), Thom Neale and Paul Tagliamonte

we work for the , and work on Open States.

For those who don't know, The Sunlight Foundation is a nonprofit focused on government transparency and accountability.

Sunlight brings high technology to help solve problems in presenting, distributing and reporting on Government data.

OpenStates helps bring some of the great work that's done on the federal level (stuff like GovTrack) to the state level.

Free and well documented APIs for programmers

Bulk data downloads for data analysts

An easy-to-use website to view a State's legislation

And most (technically) interesting:
a normalized dataset.

So, how does it all work?

There are actually a few projects that make OpenStates "tick"

There's the OpenStates scrapers which fetch websites and store important data.

Fun fact: As of the time of this writing, there are 176 scrapers.

We have (just from a rough count) 28 events scrapers, 52 legislator scrapers, 52 bill scrapers 38 committee scrapers 6 vote scrapers

Then, there's Billy, which stores and serves Legislative data.

Billy stores Bills, Legislators, Committees, Events, and Votes.

Objects tracked in billy:

  • 354,239 bill objects, which contain 3,347,724 actions, 617,683 versions of the legislation (drafts, final copies) and 259,725 attached documents.
  • 8,323 legislators (7,465 active and 858 inactive),
  • 66,397 events,
  • 256,468 vote objects,

We've had over 45 people help contribute to this effort at hackathons, sprints and hack days.

So, how (exactly) do we capture this data?

Our methodology is very important to us. We always maintain the integrity of the data, and provide sources.

Every night, we (very politely) scrape websites, consume APIs or download a state's bulk data, and store it onto our servers.

A second "stage" then takes that data and does some very careful matching work.

We only augment the data, we never change it.

At this stage, we do things like match names of legislators, match committees, and translate subjects into a normal set
